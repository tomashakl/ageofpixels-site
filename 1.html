DOS / Win 3.x Games

EF2000 (PC) — Big Box

About the Game

EF2000 is a landmark modern jet combat simulator by Digital Image Design. Fly the Eurofighter prototype in dynamic campaigns over Scandinavia with advanced avionics and immersive graphics.

Highlights

  • Dynamic campaign engine with evolving frontline and missions.
  • High‑fidelity avionics, 3D cockpit, and realistic flight model.
  • Multiplayer over network/modem; support for head‑tracking style padlock views.
  • Striking metal‑panel big box presentation with rivet motif.

System Requirements

  • Platform: IBM PC compatible
  • OS: MS‑DOS 5.0 or higher
  • CPU: 486DX2/66 MHz or better (Pentium recommended)
  • RAM: 8 MB
  • Graphics: SVGA VESA compatible
  • Sound: Sound Blaster compatible
  • Media: CD‑ROM
  • Input: Keyboard & Mouse (Joystick recommended)

Trivia

  • Developed by Digital Image Design (DID), creators of TFX.
  • Praised for its dynamic weather and atmospheric effects.
  • Spawned the Super EF2000 update for Windows/Glide later on.

Jane’s Advanced Tactical Fighters (PC) — Big Box

About the Game

Jane’s Advanced Tactical Fighters (ATF) is a modern air-combat simulator and the direct sequel to U.S. Navy Fighters. It features cutting-edge aircraft and globe-spanning campaigns with mission editor support.

Highlights

  • Fly stealth and experimental fighters (e.g., F-117A, F-22, B-2A, X-31, X-29, ASTOVL, Rafale C).
  • 80 missions across 3 campaigns (Egypt, France, Far East) plus multiplayer scenarios.
  • Pro Mission Creator tool for building your own missions.
  • Realistic damage effects, dynamic weather, and network/modem multiplayer.

System Requirements

  • Platform: IBM PC compatible
  • OS: MS-DOS 5.0 or higher
  • CPU: 486DX2/66 MHz (320×200) / Pentium 60 MHz (640×480)
  • RAM: 8 MB
  • Graphics: SVGA VESA compatible, 1 MB VRAM
  • Media: CD-ROM (2×)
  • Mouse/Joystick: 100% compatible
  • HDD: ~30–60 MB free

Trivia

  • Released in 1996 under the Jane’s Combat Simulations label (EA/Origin).
  • Ships with a spiral-bound First Edition manual and reference card.
  • Well regarded for its aircraft database and technical detail.

Lost in Time (PC) — Sierra Originals (Parts 1 & 2)

About the Game

Lost in Time is a two-part point-and-click adventure developed by Coktel Vision and published by Sierra. Combining live-action video sequences with pre-rendered backgrounds, it follows Doralice on a time‑travel journey to the 18th century.

Highlights

  • Two complete episodes included: Parts I & II.
  • FMV sequences and digitized actors for cinematic storytelling.
  • Puzzles involving time paradoxes and historical exploration.
  • Sierra Originals edition with standardized packaging.

System Requirements

  • Platform: IBM PC compatible
  • OS: MS‑DOS 5.0 or higher / Windows 3.1
  • CPU: 386DX/33 MHz or better
  • RAM: 4 MB
  • Graphics: VGA 256‑color
  • Sound: Sound Blaster compatible
  • Media: CD‑ROM
  • Input: Mouse

Trivia

  • Originally released in 1993 by Coktel Vision; Sierra edition from mid‑90s.
  • Among early adventure games with FMV segments.
  • Part of the Sierra Originals budget line.

Overlord (PC) — Big Box

About the Game

Overlord is a WWII squadron‑level combat flight sim by Rowan / Virgin set around the Normandy campaign. You manage pilots and missions and then take the cockpit for sorties over occupied France.

Highlights

  • Campaign tied to the D‑Day timeframe with dynamic mission generation.
  • Squadron management: pilot experience, fatigue and aircraft availability.
  • Multiple Allied fighter types and ground‑attack tasks.
  • Quick combat & training modes for immediate action.

System Requirements

  • Platform: IBM PC compatible
  • OS: MS‑DOS 5.0 or later
  • CPU: 386DX/33 MHz or better
  • RAM: 4 MB
  • Graphics: VGA (supports SVGA modes where available)
  • Sound: Sound Blaster or General MIDI compatible
  • Storage: ~20 MB free
  • Input: Keyboard & Mouse (Joystick optional)

Trivia

  • Limited edition big box included a large Battle of Normandy map and a spiral‑bound manual.
  • Released in 1994, coinciding with the 50th anniversary of D‑Day.
  • Developed by Rowan, known for other historical flight sims like Dawn Patrol and later Flying Corps.
